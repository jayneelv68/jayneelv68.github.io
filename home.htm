I am currently a first year PhD student at UC Davis under the advising of Prasant Mohapatra in the Networks lab. My research lies at the intersection of deep learning and wireless sensing where I'm interested in using computational models to formalize what it means to learn: sensing of real-world phenomenon in agriculture and remote patient monitoring. In particular, I explore ways in which sensing can be generalised from the scenario specific approach followed today.

As a network scientist, I follow a top-down approach to reverse-engineering how humans "sense". This view holds that we can understand key aspects of cognition by identifying the problem being solved and comparing how real world sensing differs from an ideal observer. By generating a formal description of the problem being solved, we can develop experiments to test the types of sensing people use in their everyday lives. As a result, this top-down approach provides us with a better understanding of sensing and lets us build machine learning models that observe more like people.
